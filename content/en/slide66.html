Slide 66: Spirals Born from a Collision

We're entering the final stretch of the thesis! Now we're shifting focus to a very specific type of spiral arm: those that aren't formed by the galaxy's internal processes, but are the direct consequence of a satellite galaxy colliding with our Milky Way.

These spiral arms are like the ripples that form in a pond when you throw a stone in. They are temporary, dynamic, and contain invaluable information about the "stone" that created them.
