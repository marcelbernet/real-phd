Slide 8: The Potential, or the Cosmic Slide

Let's get into a bit of math, but don't be scared. The gravitational potential sounds complicated, but the idea is simple: it's the map that tells you where gravity "falls" at each point. On Earth, it always points down. In a galaxy, it's more complex.

My favorite analogy is to think of it as a giant slide. The stars are like marbles sliding down this cosmic slide. The shape of the slide forces them to move in a certain way.

The trick is that the shape of the slide is defined by the distribution of mass (where the stars, the bar, the dark matter are...). Therefore, if we know the shape of the slide, we can know what the galaxy is made of.

And here comes the problem: we can't "see" the slide directly. But what we can do, thanks to Gaia, is measure how the marbles (the stars) are moving at this very moment.

Our job (the green arrow on the slide) is to do the reverse: from observing how the stars move, we try to reconstruct what shape the slide must have for them to move that way. And if we reconstruct the slide, we understand the structure of the galaxy.
