Slides 14-18: Finding the Troublemakers

This colorful plot is one of the most important in Galactic dynamics. Don't worry, it's simpler than it looks.

Imagine all the stars near us are kids on a giant merry-go-round.

  • Most kids just sit and spin in a circle. That's the big, bright yellow blob in the middle. They are "well-behaved" stars.
  • But some kids are running around, either towards the center or away from it. These form weird clumps and streams on the plot. They are the "troublemakers".

One of the biggest groups of troublemakers is called Hercules (yes, like the Disney movie, as pointed out by the image!). For years, we've been trying to figure out what's causing the Hercules stars to move so weirdly.
