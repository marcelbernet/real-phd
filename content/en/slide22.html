Slide 22: The Story of a "Silly" Idea

So, let's recap: a massive object moving through the dark matter halo (like a satellite) creates a wake that slows it down.

And what is a spiral arm? Well, in essence, it's also a big concentration of mass (stars, gas...) moving through the galaxy. So, the question was obvious: shouldn't a spiral arm also create a wake in the dark matter halo?

Origin story: This question came up during a meeting with Victor Debattista, a brilliant researcher, where we were just throwing "silly ideas" around. I blurted this one out, not thinking much of it, but it stuck with me.

Immediately after, I went to my office, pulled up a simulation I had handy, checked to see if the effect was there and... BOOM. It was there. The simulation clearly showed the spiral arms creating a response in the dark matter halo.

That "eureka" moment is the origin of this entire paper. It was one of those super fun research projects, and I had a great time exploring an idea that started almost as a joke.
