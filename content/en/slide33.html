Slide 33: Deceptive Simplicity

In this work, we decided to get to the root of the problem. We took the simplest possible model you can imagine for an interaction: a satellite galaxy that flies by very fast and from very far away. Think of it as the textbook case, the most basic one of all.

The surprise? Even in this ultra-simplified scenario, the resulting dynamics are chaotic. As you can see in the videos, the spirals that form are geometrically and mathematically very complex. They change shape, speed, they intertwine...

This led us to the main goal of this paper: if not even the simplest case behaves as we expected, it means we're missing a fundamental understanding. The goal, therefore, was to find the mathematical language, the equations, that govern these seemingly chaotic spirals.
