Slide 95: Where Do We Stand Now?

To finish, a final reflection on where we are in our field right now.

We are living in a golden age. We have observational data (like Gaia's) and computational simulations of a quality and resolution that were unthinkable just a few years ago. They are so good that, in many ways, they have surpassed our ability to explain them with "pen-and-paper" theory, that is, from first mathematical principles.

There is a gap between the complexity of the reality we observe and the simplicity of our theoretical models.

My thesis, and particularly the use of techniques like SINDy, sits precisely in this intermediate space. It's an attempt to build a bridge: using information from complex data and simulations to help theory evolve, to discover the new mathematics we need to understand the Universe.
