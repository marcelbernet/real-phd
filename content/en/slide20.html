Slide 20: The Verdict: Simple Static Models Don't Work

Armed with this new tool (the gradient-painted skeleton), we tested the classic fast and slow bar models. The result was unequivocal.

Both models failed spectacularly.

Although they could roughly reproduce the position of the main structures (the skeleton), they were completely unable to reproduce the gradients (the "colors"). The discrepancy was enormous, with a statistical tension of more than 3-sigma. In physics, this is very strong evidence that the model is wrong.

The conclusion of my second article is, therefore, that simple models of a galactic bar rotating at a constant speed are incorrect. The real physics must be more complex. One possibility, which is now gaining traction in the community, is that the bar is not rotating at a constant speed, but is slowing down over time.
