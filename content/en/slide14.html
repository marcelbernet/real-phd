Slide 14: My Method: The Phase-Space Skeleton (Article 1)

Until now, the analysis of these "lumps" was done by eye, in a subjective manner. This was a huge limitation. The motivation for my first article was: can we automate this? Can we create a tool that finds these structures objectively and reproducibly?

The solution came in two parts:

  • Wavelets: A mathematical tool that acts like a filter to detect peaks and valleys in the data. I used it to robustly identify the position of the main "bones" of our velocity map.
  • Graph Theory (BFS): Once you have the bones, you have to assemble the skeleton. I used a graph theory algorithm to connect the detected groups at different distances from the Sun, thus creating the first 3D "phase-space skeleton."

The result is a quantitative and objective representation of the main structures of the galactic disk, which revealed that the groups are more complex than we thought: they merge and split, rather than being simple, isolated structures.
