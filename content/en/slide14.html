Slide 14: The Potential, or the Cosmic Toboggan

Let's get into a bit of math, but don't panic. The gravitational potential sounds complicated, but the idea is simple: it's the map that tells you which way gravity "pulls" at every point. On Earth, it always points down. In a galaxy, it's more complex.

My favorite analogy is to think of it as a giant toboggan or slide. The stars are like marbles sliding down this cosmic toboggan. The shape of the slide forces them to move in a particular way.

The trick is that the shape of the toboggan is defined by the distribution of mass (where the stars, the bar, the dark matter are...). Therefore, if we know the shape of the toboggan, we can know what the galaxy is made of.

And here comes the problem: we can't "see" the toboggan directly. But what we *can* do, thanks to Gaia, is measure how the marbles (the stars) are moving at this very moment.

Our job (the green arrow on the slide) is to do the reverse: by observing how the stars are moving, we try to reconstruct what the shape of the toboggan must be to make them move that way. And if we reconstruct the toboggan, we understand the structure of the galaxy.
